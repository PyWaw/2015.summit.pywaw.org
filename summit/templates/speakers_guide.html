Speakers' Guide

One of the main aims of our conference is to provide quality and valuable talks to all attendees. Below we prepared a checklist that helps you make sure that you are perfectly ready to rock on PyWaw Summit’s stage.

The talk and slides tips

  • Practice, practice, practice! That’s super important to tell your talk loudly at least a few times before the day of conference.
  • You have exactly 30 min slot for your talk including Q&A session (unless you're keynote speaker). If you exceed the limit we will have to terminate your presentation.
  • Q&A session is optional. If you don’t want to have it just let us know.
  • You will be warmly introduced just before your talk. If you want to control what we will say just let us know about it at least one day before the conference.
  • Make sure your aim of presentation is clear and highlighted at the beginning of your talk.
  • Ensure your slides are legible and the font size is not too small for the people sitting at the end of the hall.
  • Avoid the wall of code in your slides. Except you have really good reason for that.
  • Don’t be stressed. All the people over there are really friendly with a good will to take the advantage of your talk!
  • However you should know that there will be about 250 people in the audience.
  • We will do our best to help you feel comfortable on the stage!

Technical facts

  • Projector’s resolution is 1366x768.
  • Projector has both VGA and HDMI ports.
  • Internet access is available through WiFi and Ethernet cable
  • There is an option to play video and music but please let us know about it at least 3 days before the conference.
  • There will be a microport used instead of regular microphone so your hands will be free to gesticulate.

If you have any questions or custom requirements to run your talk please let us know at info@pywaw.org.
